Situation: In order to raise students’ awareness of world affairs, the principal of a high school wants teachers to design a project on one of the following themes: climate change, terrorism, and discrimination. Which would be the best option in your opinion? Why?

**Candidate (Agent):**
**[Phiên bản 1] Thí sinh xuất sắc (C1):**
"Well, critically evaluating the three profound global themes to significantly raise students' awareness, I firmly believe that focusing a high school project on climate change is irrefutably the most universally relevant and urgently constructive option. Primarily, climate change is an undeniable, escalating existential crisis that directly impacts the future survival and tangible daily environments of this younger generation, thereby naturally fostering deep personal engagement and actively empowering them to adopt practical, eco-friendly lifestyle habits. Furthermore, it comprehensively integrates diverse academic disciplines, from environmental science to global economics, making it a highly robust project. Conversely, tackling terrorism, while undeniably critical, involves incredibly dark, violent, and intensely politically polarizing complexities that might induce severe psychological anxiety or despair rather than constructive proactive action. Similarly, while fighting discrimination is strictly essential for social justice, its highly highly sensitive, nuanced nature can sometimes inadvertently spark volatile emotional discord in a diverse classroom if not facilitated with extraordinary pedagogical expertise. Therefore, climate change is undeniably the safest, most universally unifying educational topic."
