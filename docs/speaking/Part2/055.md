Situation: You are giving a short talk on language learning. You will decide which activity is the best way to learn a new language. There are THREE options for you to choose: joining a class, self-study, and talking to native speakers. Which one would you choose? Why?

**Candidate (Agent):**
**[Phiên bản 1] Thí sinh xuất sắc (C1):**
"Well, analyzing the three proposed methods for acquiring a new language, I passionately assert that talking to native speakers is unequivocally the most effective and enriching approach. Primarily, engaging in authentic, real-time conversations provides an unparalleled linguistic immersion that effortlessly enhances pronunciation, fluidly expands practical vocabulary, and significantly boosts one's conversational confidence—elements that are notoriously missing in textbook learning. Joining a formal class, while structurally beneficial, can sometimes be overly rigorous and painfully slow, focusing too heavily on abstract grammar rules rather than immediate practical usage. Self-study, though highly flexible and cost-effective, severely lacks immediate corrective feedback, meaning a solitary learner could easily fossilize critical pronunciation errors for years without ever realizing it. Therefore, actively communicating with locals is undeniably my definitive recommendation."

Tham gia một lớp học chính quy, mặc dù có lợi về mặt cấu trúc, nhưng đôi khi có thể quá khắt khe và chậm chạp một cách đau đớn, tập trung quá nhiều vào các quy tắc ngữ pháp trừu tượng thay vì sử dụng thực tế ngay lập tức. Do đó, việc tích cực giao tiếp với người dân địa phương chắc chắn là khuyến nghị dứt khoát của tôi.)*
