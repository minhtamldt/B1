## 📄 Tình huống thảo luận (Situation)

> Situation: An optional subject will be added to the curriculum for secondary students in your city. Three options are suggested: photography, swimming, and dancing. Which one do you think is the best option for secondary students? Why?

---

## 📝 Bước 1: Nắm bắt tình huống và chọn giải pháp

- **Tình huống:** Một môn học tự chọn sẽ được thêm vào chương trình học dành cho học sinh THCS ở thành phố tôi.
- **Ba phương án:** (1) Nhiếp ảnh, (2) Bơi lội, (3) Khiêu vũ.
- **Quyết định:** ✅ Chọn **Swimming (Bơi lội)** — kỹ năng sống thiết yếu, kết hợp sức khoẻ thể chất và an toàn.

---

## 🧠 Bước 2: Chuẩn bị lý do (Bảo vệ và Bác bỏ)

**Bảo vệ — Swimming:**
- Bơi lội là kỹ năng sống quan trọng — biết bơi có thể cứu mạng sống trong tình huống khẩn cấp.
- Rèn luyện thể lực toàn diện, giúp học sinh khoẻ mạnh, giảm stress học tập.
- C1: Nhiều học sinh THCS chưa biết bơi — đây là khoảng trống cần lấp đầy ngay trong độ tuổi này.

**Bác bỏ — Photography:** Giá trị sáng tạo nhưng không có lợi ích sức khoẻ; đây là sở thích có thể tự học ngoài giờ.
**Bác bỏ — Dancing:** Vui nhộn nhưng không phải kỹ năng sống thiết yếu; không phù hợp với tất cả học sinh.

## 🎤 Bước 4: Thực hiện làm bài trả lời mẫu

### 🏆 [Phiên bản 1] Thí sinh xuất sắc (Trình độ C1)

My situation is that an optional subject is being added to the curriculum for secondary students in my city. The three options are photography, swimming, and dancing. In my opinion, **swimming** is by far the best choice, and I'd like to explain my reasoning.

First and foremost, swimming is a **life skill**, not merely a hobby. Knowing how to swim can be the difference between life and death in emergency situations — whether it's an unexpected fall into water or a natural disaster. In a country like Vietnam, which is surrounded by rivers, lakes, and coastal areas, this skill is especially vital. Teaching it during secondary school ensures that **all students** — regardless of their background — have the opportunity to acquire it.

Beyond safety, swimming is also an **outstanding form of physical exercise**. It develops cardiovascular endurance, strengthens muscles, and is known to significantly reduce stress levels — something that secondary school students, who are under considerable academic pressure, would greatly benefit from.

By comparison, photography, while creative and valuable, is a skill that students can easily **self-study** outside of school through online tutorials and practice. Dancing, similarly, is enjoyable but less universally applicable and doesn't address the critical issue of **water safety** and physical health. Neither provides the life-saving, health-enhancing value that swimming does.

To sum up, swimming is the option that offers the greatest **practical, physical, and safety benefits** for secondary students. I strongly recommend it as the chosen subject.

### 🛡️ [Phiên bản 2] Thí sinh chống liệt (Trình độ B1)

Well, my situation is that an optional subject will be added to the curriculum for secondary students in my city. Three options are suggested: photography, swimming, and dancing.

In my opinion, **swimming** is the best option because of reasons.

Firstly, swimming is good and suitable for secondary students. It is very useful and convenient because students learn a very important life skill. Swimming helps students stay healthy and also keeps them safe near water. All students can benefit from learning how to swim.

Secondly, I don't choose photography and dancing because they are not as useful and important. Photography and dancing are nice hobbies but they are not life skills. Students can learn those things by themselves outside of school.

Finally, to sum up, for my way of thinking, I would choose **swimming** for this situation. Thank you.
