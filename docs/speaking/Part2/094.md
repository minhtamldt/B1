Situation: Your niece will turn 12 next week and you want to buy her a gift. Three options are suggested: a dress, an English book, and a bouquet of flowers. Which gift do you think is the best choice? Why?

**Candidate (Agent):**
**[Phiên bản 1] Thí sinh xuất sắc (C1):**
"Well, carefully exploring the three gift options for my twelve-year-old niece, I unequivocally believe that presenting her with an engaging English book is the most profoundly enriching choice. First and foremost, navigating early adolescence is a crucial developmental stage, and an age-appropriate book simultaneously cultivates her linguistic proficiency, vastly expands her robust imaginative horizons, and provides enduring, irreplaceable intellectual value. Conversely, providing a dress, while momentarily delightful, is highly risky due to unpredictable growth spurts and rapidly vacillating teenage fashion preferences, making it inevitably outgrown or hastily discarded. A bouquet of flowers, although undeniably aesthetically pleasing, is tragically ephemeral and completely lacks the enduring, cognitive utility that a tangible educational resource reliably offers. Therefore, an inspiring book is irrefutably my ultimate decision."
