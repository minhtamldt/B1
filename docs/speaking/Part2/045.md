Situation: A student wants to find a part-time job where he can practice his English. There are THREE options to choose from: working at a souvenir shop, working at a travel agency, and working at a take-away shop. Which job would be best for him? Why?

**Candidate (Agent):**
**[Phiên bản 1] Thí sinh xuất sắc (C1):**
"Well, considering the three part-time job options aimed at improving English proficiency, I firmly advocate that working at a travel agency would be the most incredibly enriching choice. First and foremost, a travel agency environment necessitates constant, in-depth communication with foreign tourists, forcing the student to fluidly discuss complex itineraries, resolve sophisticated booking issues, and actively elevate both their listening and speaking fluency. Furthermore, it exposes them to formal professional vocabulary and diverse global accents, acting as a profound linguistic training ground. I definitely wouldn't recommend working at a souvenir shop because the interactions there are highly repetitive and overly transactional, mostly limited to merely greeting customers and negotiating simple prices. Regarding a take-away shop, the extremely fast-paced and intensely chaotic atmosphere leaves virtually no room for any meaningful, extended conversations with customers. Therefore, a travel agency unequivocally remains the most sensible decision for mastering English."
