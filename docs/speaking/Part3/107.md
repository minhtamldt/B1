Topic: There are many causes of telling lies.
Telling lies
- To get out of troubles
- To avoid embarrassment
- To protect privacy
- Your own ideas
Follow-up questions:
1. How often do you lie?
2. What’s the biggest lie you’ve ever told?
3. Some people say that children who lie well grow up to be intelligent adults. Were you a good liar when you were a child?

"While honesty is definitively a revered virtue, there are numerous profound psychological catalysts that invariably drive people to fabricate the truth. Principally, individuals lie to desperately get out of troubles, instinctively dodging severe occupational or legal consequences when they have committed a grave error. Moreover, a tremendously common motive is to gracefully avoid embarrassment; people often construct elaborate falsehoods to heavily disguise their profound insecurities or humiliating personal failures. Additionally, deception is frequently deployed as a formidable shield to firmly protect privacy from intrusive interrogations. From my perspective, we must also acknowledge the 'white lie', which is benevolently orchestrated solely to meticulously spare a loved one's fragile feelings. Ultimately, although inherently problematic, lying is an incredibly complex, deeply fascinating human defense mechanism."
