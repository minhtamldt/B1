"While the necessity of school discipline is undeniable, the assertion that punishment is inherently good for students is highly debatable. Proponents often argue that fear of punitive measures forces students to obey teachers and maintain order in the classroom. Consequently, a strict environment theoretically helps students concentrate better on their studies by eliminating distractions. Furthermore, establishing clear consequences might minimize blatant misbehaviour among rebellious youths. However, from my personal perspective, constructive discipline, such as counseling or withdrawing privileges, is vastly superior to harsh punishment. Severe punitive actions often breed resentment, damage a student's self-esteem, and fail to address the root causes of their behavioral issues. Therefore, while maintaining discipline is crucial, the methods should be restorative rather than purely punitive."
