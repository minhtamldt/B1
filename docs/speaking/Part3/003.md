"Today, I'd like to share my thoughts on the profound benefits of pair work and how it fundamentally enhances the learning experience for students at school. First and foremost, collaborating with a partner provides a valuable opportunity to learn from one another. By explaining concepts or tackling complex problems together, students can gain diverse perspectives and reinforce their own understanding. Moreover, working in pairs effectively encourages students to become far more active and engaged in the classroom. Instead of passively listening to lectures, they actively participate in dialogue and problem-solving. Furthermore, this collaborative approach significantly improves their social skills and interaction, teaching them vital interpersonal competencies such as active listening, compromise, and constructive feedback. To sum up, I am convinced that pair work is an indispensable pedagogical tool."

**Question 2: What are some possible disadvantages of working in pairs?**
**[Phiên bản 1] Thí sinh xuất sắc (C1):** 
"One major drawback is the potential for unequal contribution, commonly known as 'social loafing', where one student ends up doing the lion's share of the work while the other slacks off. Additionally, if the pairs aren't carefully matched, it can lead to personality clashes and counterproductive arguments."
*(Bản dịch tiếng Việt: Một nhược điểm lớn là nguy cơ đóng góp không đồng đều, thường được gọi là 'lười biếng xã hội', nơi một học sinh vô tình đảm nhận phần lớn công việc trong khi người kia thì lười nhác. Thêm vào đó, nếu các cặp không được ghép đôi cẩn thận, nó có thể dẫn đến xung đột tính cách và những cuộc tranh cãi phản tác dụng.)*
