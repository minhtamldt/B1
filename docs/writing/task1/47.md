Thank you for writing to me. It is very nice that you want to organise a Mid-Autumn Festival for Vietnamese students. I am happy to help!

The Mid-Autumn Festival is called Tet Trung Thu in Vietnamese. Vietnamese children celebrate it by carrying colourful lanterns and walking in the streets at night. This is very beautiful and fun. They also watch lion dances. The lion dance is very exciting and it is believed to bring good luck.

For gifts, children usually receive lanterns and small toys. Parents and grandparents also give children money to buy things they like.

The most important food of the festival is mooncakes. In Vietnamese, we call them banh trung thu. They have many different fillings inside like lotus seeds or green beans. We eat mooncakes and give them to our family and friends.
