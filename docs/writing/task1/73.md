What a wonderful project! I am more than happy to share some information about our most beloved festival for your presentation.

Without a doubt, the most important and widely celebrated festival in Vietnam is Tết Nguyên Đán, or simply Tết — the Vietnamese Lunar New Year. It typically falls between late January and mid-February, with celebrations spanning anywhere from one to two weeks, though the official public holiday lasts for around five days.

Tết holds an extraordinary cultural significance in Vietnam, as it marks not only the beginning of a new lunar year, but also the occasion for family reunion, ancestor veneration, and the renewal of hopes for the year ahead. It is, in essence, the single most emotionally meaningful time of the year for Vietnamese people.

During Tết, people traditionally spend days before the holiday thoroughly cleaning their homes to sweep away bad luck, decorating with peach blossoms and kumquat trees, and preparing elaborate feasts. Giving lucky money in red envelopes to children and elders is a cherished custom, and spectacular firework shows at midnight on New Year’s Eve light up cities across the country.

Dear Linda,

Thank you for your email. I am very happy to tell you about the most popular festival in Vietnam!

The most popular festival in Vietnam is Tet Nguyen Dan, or Tet for short. It is the Vietnamese New Year. Tet usually starts in late January or early February. It lasts for about one or two weeks, but the main holiday is around five days.

Tet is very important in Vietnam because it is the time for family reunion. Vietnamese people believe that the way you start the new year will decide how the whole year goes. So everyone tries to be happy and kind during Tet.

During Tet, Vietnamese people clean their houses to remove bad luck. They decorate their homes with peach flowers and small orange trees. They also cook special food and give children red envelopes with lucky money inside. At midnight on New Year's Eve, there are beautiful fireworks shows all over the country.

If you come to Vietnam for Tet one day, I will definitely join you!

Warm wishes,
