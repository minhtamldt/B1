Dear Dan,

I was absolutely delighted to receive your postcard! It sounds like you are incredibly busy preparing for Christmas. To answer your first question, although Christmas is not a traditional public holiday in Vietnam, a lot of young people still celebrate it by decorating pine trees and exchanging gifts.

You also asked about our traditional holidays. In Vietnam, we celebrate several major milestones, but the most important and widely-celebrated one is definitely the Lunar New Year, locally known as Tet. During this festive season, families gather together for a massive reunion dinner. We typically spend time cleaning and decorating our houses with peach blossoms or apricot blossoms to welcome good luck. Additionally, children are often given "lucky money" in red envelopes, which is always the most exciting part for them.

Since you are getting ready for Christmas, I have a few questions about British holidays. What is your favorite dish during Christmas? Besides Christmas, what other traditional festivals do you celebrate in the UK? Do you usually travel during these holidays or stay at home with your family?

I can't wait to hear from you soon. 

Lots of love,
