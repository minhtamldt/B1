🏆 **2. BÀI MẪU C1 (~305 Từ)**

The role of sport within the school curriculum has long been a subject of robust pedagogical debate. Advocates for mandatory physical education argue compellingly that regular sport participation confers a broad spectrum of developmental benefits that extend far beyond the playing field, while those who favour an optional approach raise legitimate concerns about individual autonomy and educational breadth. On balance, I believe that sports should be a compulsory component of school life, with appropriate accommodations for individual differences.

The most persuasive argument in favour of mandatory school sport lies in its multidimensional developmental impact. Numerous peer-reviewed studies confirm that regular physical activity in childhood and adolescence enhances cardiovascular health, fortifies mental resilience, and measurably improves cognitive function and academic concentration. Beyond the purely physical dimension, team sports cultivate essential transferable competencies — communication, leadership, conflict resolution, and perseverance under pressure — that no academic subject can replicate with the same experiential immediacy. For many students, sport also provides a vital sense of belonging and community identity within the school environment.

However, the counterargument that sport should be purely elective merits serious consideration. Students with physical disabilities, health conditions, or divergent interests deserve an educational environment that honours their individuality. Forcing disengaged students to participate in sports they loathe can breed resentment and anxiety, undermining both their well-being and their academic motivation.

The most sensible resolution is a structured compromise: schools should mandate broad physical education classes that offer diverse sporting options, while allowing students to choose activities aligned with their abilities and interests. Exemptions should be available for genuine medical or psychological reasons.

In conclusion, sport undeniably enriches school life and should be embedded within the curriculum, but its implementation must be sufficiently inclusive to serve all students equitably.

🛟 **3. BÀI MẪU B1 (~260 Từ)**

Sports have always been an important part of school life. However, there is a debate about whether sports should be a mandatory subject for all students or whether they should be optional. In my opinion, sports should be a required part of school education, but schools should also offer different activities to suit different students.

First, those who support mandatory sports argue that physical activity is very important for children’s health. Regular exercise helps students stay fit, reduces stress, and improves concentration in class. Research shows that students who are physically active tend to perform better in academic subjects. In addition, team sports teach students important life skills such as teamwork, communication, and leadership, which are very useful in the future.

However, some people believe that sports should be optional. They argue that not all students enjoy sports. Some students may have physical disabilities or health problems that prevent them from participating. Others may prefer to spend their free time on art, music, or other activities. Forcing all students to play sports could make some of them unhappy or anxious.

In my view, the best solution is to make physical education compulsory, but to offer a wide variety of activities so that every student can find something they enjoy. Schools should also provide exemptions for students with medical conditions.

In conclusion, sports should be a required part of school life because of the many health and social benefits they provide. However, the programme should be flexible enough to include all students comfortably.
