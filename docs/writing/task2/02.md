In recent years, juvenile delinquency has emerged as a deeply troubling social phenomenon that demands urgent attention. While the causes are multifaceted, a closer examination reveals that they are largely rooted in environmental and socio-economic factors, and that effective solutions must be implemented at multiple levels of society.

The most significant cause of teenage crime is arguably the breakdown of the family unit. When young people grow up in households characterised by conflict, neglect, or poverty, they are far more likely to seek belonging and validation in harmful peer groups or gangs. A teenager who has never been taught the value of empathy or self-discipline at home is ill-equipped to make responsible moral choices. Furthermore, the pervasive influence of violent media content, from video games to social media platforms, normalises aggressive behaviour and desensitises young minds to the consequences of crime.

Another crucial contributing factor is inadequate education. When schools fail to engage disenchanted students, dropout rates rise, leaving adolescents with excessive unsupervised time and limited future prospects. Without meaningful goals, crime can appear an attractive shortcut to financial gain or social status.

To address this crisis effectively, governments must pursue a dual strategy. First, robust family support programmes — including parenting workshops, mental health counselling, and financial assistance for low-income households — can strengthen the foundational environment in which children develop. Second, educational reforms that make learning more inclusive and vocational training more accessible could provide at-risk youth with legitimate pathways to success. Community youth centres that offer mentorship, recreational activities, and life-skills coaching have also proven remarkably effective in redirecting vulnerable teenagers toward positive outcomes.

In conclusion, while teenage crime is a complex issue, its root causes are identifiable and its solutions are within reach. A society that invests in its young people through supportive families, quality education, and strong communities will ultimately produce fewer criminals and more responsible citizens.

🛟 **3. BÀI MẪU B1 (~260 Từ)**

Nowadays, teenage crime is a serious problem in many countries. There are many reasons for this, and we need to find good solutions to deal with it.

First, one of the main causes of teenage crime is family problems. Many young people come from broken or poor families where they do not receive enough care and guidance from their parents. Without proper support at home, teenagers may feel lost and start doing wrong things. For example, they may join bad groups of friends and start stealing or fighting.

Second, the bad influence of the internet and social media is another important cause. Many teenagers spend a lot of time watching violent videos or playing violent games. This can make them think that violence is normal and acceptable.

Third, dropping out of school is also a big reason. When young people leave school early, they have nothing to do and no future plans. They may turn to crime to make money or to feel important.

To solve this problem, I think we need several solutions. Governments should provide support for families with financial difficulties so that parents can take better care of their children. Schools should also create interesting programmes to keep students engaged and lower dropout rates. Additionally, local community centres should offer free activities for young people, such as sports, music, or volunteering, to keep them busy and out of trouble.

In conclusion, teenage crime is a complex problem caused by family issues, bad media influence, and poor education. With the right support from families, schools, and communities, we can help young people make better choices and reduce crime.
