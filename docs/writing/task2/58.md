🏆 **2. BÀI MẪU C1 (~305 Từ)**

The assertion that today’s young people face significantly greater pressures than their parents’ generation is one that I find convincingly supported by the realities of contemporary life. While it is true that previous generations endured their own formidable hardships — economic scarcity, war, and limited opportunity — the nature and volume of pressures confronting young people today are arguably both more complex and more pervasive.

Perhaps the most distinctive and historically unprecedented source of pressure facing modern youth is the ubiquitous influence of social media. Unlike their parents, who could leave the social dynamics of school at the school gate, today’s teenagers carry the pressures of peer comparison, cyberbullying, and the relentless performance of a curated identity into every waking hour. The algorithmically amplified exposure to idealised images of wealth, beauty, and success creates a near-impossible standard of comparison that has been empirically linked to alarming rates of anxiety, depression, and body dysmorphia among young people worldwide.

Furthermore, the academic and professional landscape young people navigate today is considerably more competitive than in previous decades. University entrance requires not merely academic achievement but an impressive portfolio of extracurricular activities, internships, and digital skills. The simultaneous explosion of global job competition, driven by automation and globalisation, means that career security — which previous generations could often take for granted — is now a genuine source of existential anxiety for many young adults.

The climate crisis represents yet another unprecedented burden borne disproportionately by today’s youth, who face the psychological weight of an uncertain environmental future not of their making.

In conclusion, while every generation faces unique challenges, the cumulative and interconnected pressures confronting today’s young people are genuinely exceptional in both their intensity and their novelty.

🛟 **3. BÀI MẪU B1 (~258 Từ)**

Some people think that young people today face more pressure than previous generations, while others believe that modern life is actually easier. I agree that young people today face much greater pressure than their parents and grandparents did.

First, social media has created a new kind of pressure that did not exist before. Teenagers today spend a lot of time on platforms like Instagram and TikTok, where they constantly compare themselves to others. They feel pressure to look attractive, be popular, and share exciting content. This can lead to anxiety, low self-esteem, and even depression. In the past, young people did not have to deal with this kind of pressure.

Second, academic competition has become much more intense. In the past, getting a good education was enough to find a stable job. Today, students must not only get top grades but also participate in clubs, internships, and extra activities to stand out. The job market is much more competitive because of technology and globalisation, which means even well-qualified graduates struggle to find good jobs.

Third, young people today also worry about serious global problems like climate change, economic uncertainty, and social unrest. These are issues that their parents did not have to think about when they were young.

In conclusion, while previous generations also had difficulties, young people today face a unique combination of social, academic, and global pressures that are more intense and complex than those faced by earlier generations.
