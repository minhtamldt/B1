🏆 **2. BÀI MẪU C1 (~305 Từ)**

The contention that learning art is an unproductive use of time and that children should solely concentrate on mathematics and languages is a reductive viewpoint that ignores the holistic nature of human development. While the importance of core academic subjects is indisputable, I strongly disagree with the idea that artistic education is a "waste of time."

Firstly, art education fosters critical thinking and problem-solving skills that are essential in the modern workforce. Unlike many traditional subjects that follow rigid formulas, art encourages divergent thinking—the ability to approach a problem from multiple perspectives and find innovative solutions. This creative capacity is highly valued in fields as diverse as engineering, marketing, and software design. By engaging with art, children learn to tolerate ambiguity and take risks, which are vital traits for future leaders and innovators.

Secondly, art plays a crucial role in emotional and psychological well-being. It provides children with a unique medium for self-expression and a healthy outlet for processing complex emotions. In an increasingly high-pressure educational environment, artistic activities can serve as a form of stress relief, promoting mental health and resilience. Furthermore, studying art history and global traditions fosters cultural empathy and a deeper understanding of human diversity, which are essential competencies in our globalised world.

Of course, mathematics and languages are fundamental building blocks for communication and logic. However, they should not be taught in isolation. A well-rounded education includes both the analytical rigour of the sciences and the creative exploration of the arts. They are complementary disciplines that together produce well-adjusted, versatile individuals.

In conclusion, I firmly disagree that art is a waste of time. Far from being a distraction, it is an essential component of a comprehensive education that prepares children for both professional success and personal fulfilment.

🛟 **3. BÀI MẪU B1 (~255 Từ)**

Some people believe that children should not spend time learning art because it is not useful. They think children should focus only on important subjects like math and languages. I strongly disagree with this opinion. I believe that art is very important for children's growth.

First of all, art helps children develop their creativity and imagination. When children draw or paint, they learn how to create new things and use their minds in a different way than in a math class. This creativity is very useful for their future jobs because every company needs people who can think of new ideas. Learning art also helps children improve their hand-eye coordination and concentration.

Second, art is a great way for children to express their feelings. Sometimes children find it hard to talk about their emotions, but they can express them through colors and pictures. This makes them feel happier and less stressed at school. In addition, learning about art from different countries helps children understand more about the world and other cultures.

It is true that math and languages are very important for getting a job and communicating with others. However, school should be a place where children learn many different things. If they only study math and languages, they will find school very boring and might lose interest in learning.

In conclusion, I think art is definitely not a waste of time. It is a necessary part of education that helps children become more creative, happy, and well-rounded people. Schools should continue to teach art alongside math and languages.
