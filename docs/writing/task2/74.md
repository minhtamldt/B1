# 📝 Đề thi Writing Task 2 (Bài Luận / Essay)

> **⏱️ Thời gian làm bài:** Khoảng 40 phút
> **✍️ Độ dài yêu cầu:** Ít nhất 250 từ
> **🎯 Tiêu chí đánh giá:** Task Fulfillment, Organization, Vocabulary, Grammar

--- 

## 📄 Nội dung đề bài

> Read the following text about the global economy.
>
> Globalization refers to the increasing interconnection of the world's economies, cultures, and populations. Over the past few decades, this process has accelerated dramatically due to improvements in technology and international trade. While many see globalization as a positive force for development, others are concerned about its serious disadvantages.

**Yêu cầu:**
Write an essay for an educated audience discussing the advantages and disadvantages of globalization. You should write at least 250 words. Your response will be evaluated in terms of Task Fulfillment, Organization, Vocabulary and Grammar.

---

## 💡 Gợi ý giải bài (Agent)

### Bước 1: Phân tích đề bài

- **Chủ đề (Topic):** Toàn cầu hóa (Globalization) — sự kết nối ngày càng tăng giữa các nền kinh tế và văn hóa thế giới.
- **Dạng bài (Type):** Advantages and Disadvantages (Lợi ích và Tác hại).
- **Nhiệm vụ (Task):**
  1. Phân tích những lợi ích của toàn cầu hóa (phát triển kinh tế, chia sẻ văn hóa, khoa học).
  2. Phân tích những tác hại (mất bản sắc văn hóa, bất bình đẳng, ô nhiễm môi trường).

### Bước 2: Dàn ý (Outline)

- **Mở bài (Introduction):**
  - Khái quát: Toàn cầu hóa là một trong những xu hướng nổi bật nhất của thế kỷ 21.
  - Thesis statement: Bài viết sẽ phân tích cả hai mặt tích cực và tiêu cực của tiến trình này.

- **Thân bài (Body):**
  - **Đoạn 1 (Ưu điểm):**
    - Thúc đẩy thương mại và tăng trưởng kinh tế: các quốc gia đang phát triển có thêm thị trường, đầu tư nước ngoài và việc làm.
    - Trao đổi văn hóa và khoa học: tiếp cận y tế tiên tiến, giáo dục chất lượng cao, giao lưu văn hóa làm giàu tư duy.
  - **Đoạn 2 (Nhược điểm):**
    - Mai một bản sắc văn hóa địa phương: văn hóa phương Tây (nhạc, phim, đồ ăn nhanh) lấn át truyền thống địa phương.
    - Bất bình đẳng: các tập đoàn lớn hưởng lợi nhiều hơn, trong khi lao động nghèo bị ép giá; khoảng cách giàu nghèo ngày càng lớn.

- **Kết bài (Conclusion):**
  - Tóm tắt: Toàn cầu hóa mang lại cơ hội kinh tế to lớn nhưng cũng gây ra những thách thức văn hóa và xã hội. Cần quản lý khéo léo để đảm bảo lợi ích được chia sẻ công bằng.

### Bước 3: Bài mẫu xuất sắc (Trình độ B2-C1)

Globalization, characterized by the deepening integration of economies, cultures, and populations across national borders, has fundamentally reshaped the way the world operates. Driven by rapid technological advancement and the liberalization of international trade, this process has generated both remarkable opportunities and significant concerns. This essay examines both dimensions of globalization objectively.

On the one hand, globalization has delivered substantial economic and intellectual benefits. Perhaps most visibly, it has created an expansive global marketplace that enables developing nations to attract foreign investment, access larger consumer markets, and generate employment opportunities for their populations. Countries that have actively embraced globalization, such as Vietnam and South Korea, have experienced dramatic improvements in living standards over relatively short periods. Beyond economics, globalization has accelerated the cross-border exchange of scientific knowledge, medical innovations, and educational resources, allowing breakthroughs in one country to rapidly benefit populations worldwide.

On the other hand, globalization carries notable disadvantages that demand careful consideration. A pressing concern is the gradual erosion of cultural diversity. As Western media, fast food chains, and consumer brands dominate global markets, local languages, traditions, and artistic expressions face the risk of being marginalized or lost entirely. Furthermore, the economic gains of globalization have not been distributed equally. Multinational corporations frequently exploit cheap labor in developing countries, suppressing wages while accumulating vast profits. As a result, the gap between wealthy and impoverished populations, both within and between nations, has widened considerably.

In conclusion, globalization is a double-edged force: it drives economic growth and knowledge-sharing while simultaneously threatening cultural identity and deepening inequality. Governments must therefore implement fair trade policies and cultural protection measures to ensure that its benefits are shared more equitably.

*(295 words)*

**Từ vựng & Cấu trúc "ăn điểm":**
- **Deepening integration:** Sự hội nhập ngày càng sâu rộng.
- **Liberalization of international trade:** Việc tự do hóa thương mại quốc tế.
- **Dramatic improvements in living standards:** Những cải thiện đáng kể trong mức sống.
- **Cross-border exchange:** Trao đổi xuyên biên giới.
- **Erosion of cultural diversity:** Sự xói mòn sự đa dạng văn hóa.
- **Multinational corporations:** Các tập đoàn đa quốc gia.
- **Double-edged force:** Một lực lượng hai mặt (như con dao hai lưỡi).

### Bước 4: Bài mẫu an toàn (Trình độ B1)

Globalization means that the countries of the world are becoming more and more connected to each other through trade, technology, and culture. This process has been growing very fast in recent decades. Like most big changes, globalization has both good effects and bad effects on people around the world.

On one hand, globalization has many clear advantages. First, it helps developing countries grow their economies. When foreign companies invest in poorer countries, they create jobs and help local people earn more money. For example, many factories in Vietnam and Bangladesh were built by foreign companies, giving millions of people steady work. Second, globalization allows people to share knowledge and new ideas across borders. Medical discoveries made in one country can quickly help patients in other countries. Students can also access better education and information from around the world through the internet.

On the other hand, globalization also has serious disadvantages. One major problem is that it can destroy local cultures. When powerful Western media, music, and food brands spread around the world, traditional local customs and languages may slowly disappear. Young people may prefer foreign culture over their own heritage. Another disadvantage is that globalization increases economic inequality. Large international companies often move their factories to the poorest countries to pay workers the lowest possible wages. These workers are often treated unfairly, while company bosses in rich countries become even wealthier.

In conclusion, globalization brings important benefits such as economic growth and the sharing of knowledge, but it also threatens cultural identity and increases the gap between rich and poor. Governments should manage globalization carefully so that its benefits can be enjoyed by everyone.

*(280 words)*
