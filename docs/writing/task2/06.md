🏆 **2. BÀI MẪU C1 (~310 Từ)**

The Internet has fundamentally transformed the way human beings communicate, connect, and coexist. While it has undoubtedly expanded the boundaries of human interaction on a global scale, there is growing concern that its proliferation has simultaneously eroded the depth and authenticity of our face-to-face relationships. In my view, the Internet has had both profoundly positive and deeply concerning effects on human social interactions.

On the positive side, the Internet has democratised communication, enabling individuals to maintain relationships across vast geographical distances that would previously have been insurmountable. Social media platforms, instant messaging, and video conferencing tools allow families living continents apart to celebrate birthdays together, old friends to reconnect after decades, and professionals to collaborate across time zones. For millions of migrants, international students, and expatriates, these digital bridges are nothing short of a lifeline.

However, a compelling body of research suggests that this unprecedented connectivity comes at a significant cost. When people substitute genuine in-person encounters with curated online personas, the richness of non-verbal communication is inevitably lost. Studies have found strong correlations between excessive social media use and heightened feelings of loneliness, inadequacy, and social anxiety. Furthermore, algorithmic echo chambers created by social media platforms discourage meaningful cross-cultural dialogue and polarise communities.

Ultimately, I believe the Internet is a powerful tool that, when used mindfully and in moderation, can enrich our social lives immeasurably. The challenge lies in ensuring that our online interactions complement, rather than replace, the irreplaceable warmth and authenticity of human connection in the physical world.

🛟 **3. BÀI MẪU B1 (~255 Từ)**

The Internet has become an important part of our daily lives. It has changed the way we communicate and connect with others. In my opinion, the Internet has both positive and negative effects on human interactions.

On the positive side, the Internet helps people stay connected with friends and family who live far away. For example, people can use social media, chat apps, or video calls to talk with their loved ones at any time. This is especially helpful for people who work or study in another country.

Another positive effect is that the Internet allows us to meet new people from different countries. We can join online communities to share interests and make new friends. This makes the world feel more connected.

However, the Internet also has negative effects. Many people spend too much time on social media instead of meeting real people. So they may feel lonely even though they have many online friends. Research also shows that too much social media use can cause anxiety and low self-esteem, especially in young people.

In conclusion, the Internet can improve our social lives if we use it wisely. We should use it to support our real relationships, not to replace them. Spending time with people face to face is still the most important way to build strong friendships.
