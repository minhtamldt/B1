> **⏱️ Thời gian làm bài:** Khoảng 40 phút
> **✍️ Độ dài yêu cầu:** Ít nhất 250 từ
> **🎯 Tiêu chí đánh giá:** Task Fulfillment, Organization, Vocabulary, Grammar

## 📄 Nội dung đề bài

> Read the following text about family life.
>
> Living in an extended family, where multiple generations share one home, is a traditional practice in many cultures. While this arrangement is considered old-fashioned by some, others believe it brings significant benefits. However, it also comes with clear challenges for the people involved.

**Yêu cầu:**
Write an essay for an educated audience discussing the advantages and disadvantages of living in an extended family. You should write at least 250 words. Your response will be evaluated in terms of Task Fulfillment, Organization, Vocabulary and Grammar.

---

## 💡 Gợi ý giải bài (Agent)

### Bước 1: Phân tích đề bài

- **Chủ đề (Topic):** Sống cùng đại gia đình (Extended family living) — nhiều thế hệ chung sống dưới một mái nhà.
- **Dạng bài (Type):** Advantages and Disadvantages (Lợi ích và Tác hại).
- **Nhiệm vụ (Task):**
  1. Phân tích những lợi ích của việc sống cùng đại gia đình (hỗ trợ nhau về tài chính, tình cảm, truyền thống).
  2. Phân tích những bất lợi (mâu thuẫn thế hệ, thiếu không gian riêng tư).

### Bước 2: Dàn ý (Outline)

- **Mở bài (Introduction):**
  - Khái quát: Mô hình đại gia đình vẫn phổ biến ở nhiều nơi, đặc biệt tại Châu Á. Tuy nhiên, nó mang cả ưu lẫn nhược điểm.
  - Thesis statement: Bài viết sẽ phân tích cả hai mặt của hình thức sống này.

- **Thân bài (Body):**
  - **Đoạn 1 (Ưu điểm):**
    - Hỗ trợ tài chính và chia sẻ chi phí sinh hoạt → giảm gánh nặng kinh tế, nhất là cho các cặp vợ chồng trẻ.
    - Hệ thống chăm sóc tự nhiên: ông bà trông cháu, con cháu phụng dưỡng cha mẹ già, không cần thuê ngoài.
  - **Đoạn 2 (Nhược điểm):**
    - Mâu thuẫn giữa các thế hệ do quan điểm, lối sống, giá trị khác nhau → xung đột thường xuyên.
    - Thiếu không gian riêng tư cho các thành viên, đặc biệt là vợ chồng trẻ → căng thẳng và mất tự do.

- **Kết bài (Conclusion):**
  - Tóm tắt: Sống cùng đại gia đình có thể rất bổ trợ nhưng đòi hỏi sự tôn trọng lẫn nhau và giao tiếp cởi mở để tránh xung đột.

### Bước 3: Bài mẫu xuất sắc (Trình độ B2-C1)

The concept of multi-generational households, in which grandparents, parents, and children coexist under a single roof, has deep cultural roots in many societies, particularly across Asia and the Middle East. Although this traditional arrangement is increasingly questioned in the face of modernizing lifestyles, it continues to offer notable benefits alongside undeniable drawbacks. This essay explores both dimensions.

On the one hand, extended family living provides significant practical and emotional advantages. Economically, pooling household resources allows family members to share the financial burdens of rent, food, and utilities, which is especially valuable for young couples struggling with rising living costs. Beyond financial relief, the family unit naturally functions as a built-in support network. Grandparents can assume childcare responsibilities, thereby freeing up parents to pursue careers, while adult children can reciprocate by providing physical and emotional support for elderly relatives. This organic system of mutual care reduces dependence on expensive external services and strengthens family bonds across generations.

On the other hand, co-habitation across generations frequently generates notable friction. Divergent values, lifestyle habits, and expectations between older and younger generations can lead to persistent disagreements over daily decisions, parenting approaches, and household management. Such conflicts, if left unresolved, can create a stressful home atmosphere that negatively affects everyone's well-being. Furthermore, privacy becomes a significant concern. Newly married couples, in particular, may find it difficult to establish their own identity and routines when constantly surrounded by relatives, potentially creating tension within the marriage.

In conclusion, living with an extended family offers considerable economic and caregiving benefits but also presents challenges related to generational conflict and personal privacy. Whether this arrangement succeeds ultimately depends on mutual respect, clear communication, and the willingness of all members to adapt.

*(302 words)*

**Từ vựng & Cấu trúc "ăn điểm":**
- **Multi-generational households:** Hộ gia đình đa thế hệ.
- **Pooling household resources:** Gộp chung các nguồn lực hộ gia đình.
- **Built-in support network:** Mạng lưới hỗ trợ tự nhiên/sẵn có.
- **Reciprocate:** Đáp lại, có đi có lại.
- **Organic system of mutual care:** Hệ thống chăm sóc lẫn nhau hữu cơ/tự nhiên.
- **Divergent values:** Các giá trị khác biệt/phân kỳ.
- **Persistent disagreements:** Những bất đồng dai dẳng.

### Bước 4: Bài mẫu an toàn (Trình độ B1)

In many countries, especially in Asia, it is common for grandparents, parents, and children to live together in the same house. This type of living arrangement is called an extended family. Living this way has both good points and bad points, which I will discuss in this essay.

On the one hand, there are many important benefits of living with an extended family. First, it is cheaper because everyone shares the cost of rent, food, and other bills. This is very helpful for young couples who do not earn much money yet. Second, grandparents can help look after the children while the parents go to work. This means parents do not need to spend money on childcare services. At the same time, adult children can take care of the elderly grandparents at home, which saves money on nursing homes and keeps the family close.

On the other hand, there are also some disadvantages. One common problem is that older and younger generations often think very differently about many things, such as how to raise children, how to spend money, or what rules to follow. These different opinions can easily cause arguments that make the home environment very stressful. Another problem is that young couples living with many relatives may not have enough private space for themselves. Without enough privacy, they may feel uncomfortable or unable to live freely in their own home.

In conclusion, living in an extended family can provide great financial support and a natural caregiving system. However, it can also lead to family conflicts and a lack of personal space. Good communication and mutual respect are the keys to making this kind of living arrangement work well.
