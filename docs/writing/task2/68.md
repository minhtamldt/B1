🏆 **2. BÀI MẪU C1 (~305 Từ)**

The rising prevalence of childhood obesity is a major public health concern, often attributed to the sedentary habits of modern life. Consequently, some argue that increasing the duration of physical education (PE) at school is the most effective strategy to address this issue. While I agree that enhanced PE programmes are crucial, I disagree with the notion that they constitute the "best" or "only" way to solve the problem, as obesity is a complex issue requiring a multi-dimensional approach.

Admittedly, increasing PE time has significant merits. School is the primary environment where children spend their day, and mandating regular physical activity ensures that even the most inactive students engage in exercise. Well-structured PE classes do not only help burn calories but also instill lifelong healthy habits and teamwork. Furthermore, schools can provide facilities and professional guidance that many families might lack at home. By making exercise a fundamental part of the curriculum, we can combat the allure of sedentary activities like video games and social media.

However, relying solely on PE is insufficient if a child's diet remains poor. Obesity is fundamentally a result of an imbalance between calorie intake and expenditure. No matter how much a child exercises at school, the benefits can be easily undermined by a diet rich in fast food, sugary sodas, and processed snacks. Therefore, nutritional education and stricter regulations on school canteen food are equally, if not more, important. Furthermore, the responsibility should not rest entirely on schools. Parents must be encouraged to limit screen time at home and promote active outdoor weekend activities. Without a supportive home environment, school-based interventions will have limited impact.

In conclusion, while increasing physical education is a vital component of the fight against childhood obesity, it is not a panacea. A truly effective solution must integrate improved school PE with nutritional education and active parental involvement.

🛟 **3. BÀI MẪU B1 (~255 Từ)**

Many children today are overweight because they eat too much fast food and spend too much time playing video games. Some people think that increasing physical education (PE) at school is the best way to solve this problem. In my opinion, I agree that more PE is important, but it is not the only solution.

On the one hand, having more PE classes at school is a very good idea. Every child has to go to school, so the school can make sure that all students do some exercise every day. For example, they can play sports like football, basketball, or swimming. This helps them burn energy and stay healthy. Also, if children enjoy sports at school, they might continue to play them when they grow up. The school is a great place to teach children that exercise is fun and necessary.

On the other hand, the problem of obesity is also about food. If children eat too many hamburgers, chips, and sweets, they will still be overweight even if they do exercise. Therefore, schools should also teach children about healthy eating. For example, they can provide more fruits and vegetables in the school canteen instead of soft drinks and snacks. Also, parents have to help at home. They should limit the time children spend on computers and take them to the park on weekends.

In conclusion, I think that increasing physical education is a great step, but it is not enough. To solve the problem of overweight children, we need a combination of more exercise at school, better food, and support from parents at home.
