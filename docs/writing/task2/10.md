The deteriorating quality of life in large cities has become one of the most pressing urban challenges of our time. Driven primarily by unchecked population growth and escalating pollution levels, this phenomenon demands a comprehensive and multi-pronged response from governments, urban planners, and citizens alike.

The most significant contributing factor is the relentless influx of rural migrants seeking better economic opportunities in urban centres. As cities become increasingly overcrowded, the strain on infrastructure — housing, public transport, healthcare, and sanitation — becomes untenable. Substandard housing, chronic traffic congestion, and overburdened public services are the inevitable consequences of cities growing faster than they can sustainably absorb. Compounding this crisis is the severe deterioration of air quality. Industrial emissions, vehicle exhaust, and construction dust have rendered the air in many major cities hazardous, contributing to alarming rates of respiratory disease and drastically reducing residents’ life expectancy and overall well-being.

Addressing these deeply intertwined challenges requires action on multiple fronts. First, decentralising economic development by investing heavily in secondary cities and rural infrastructure would reduce the irresistible pull of mega-cities and distribute population growth more equitably. Second, transitioning to clean energy sources and enforcing stringent emissions regulations on both industry and transport would dramatically improve urban air quality. Finally, expanding and modernising public transport networks can significantly reduce private vehicle usage, tackling both congestion and pollution simultaneously.

In conclusion, while the deterioration of urban life quality is undeniably serious, it is not irreversible. With political will, sustained investment, and coordinated urban planning, cities can be transformed into sustainable, liveable environments for all.

🛟 **3. BÀI MẪU B1 (~260 Từ)**

In recent years, the living conditions in large cities have become worse. This is a serious problem that affects many people. There are two main causes and some important solutions that we should consider.

The first cause is population explosion. Every year, many people move from the countryside to big cities looking for work and better lives. As a result, cities become too crowded. There are not enough houses, hospitals, schools, or roads for everyone. This makes life harder for everyone in the city.

The second cause is pollution. Factories, cars, and construction activities produce a lot of smoke and waste. This makes the air dirty and unpleasant to breathe. Many people in cities suffer from breathing problems because of polluted air. Water and noise pollution also make city life unpleasant.

To solve these problems, I think we need several actions. First, governments should invest more in rural areas so that people do not have to move to the city to find jobs. This will help reduce overcrowding. Second, cities should have stricter rules about pollution from factories and vehicles. Cars with high emissions should pay a lot of tax, and factories should use cleaner technology. Finally, cities should build better and cheaper public transport systems so that fewer people use private cars.

In conclusion, the quality of life in large cities is getting worse because of overcrowding and pollution. If governments and citizens work together, we can make cities cleaner and more comfortable places to live.
