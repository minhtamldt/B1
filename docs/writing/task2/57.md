🏆 **2. BÀI MẪU C1 (~300 Từ)**

The claim that computers are more harmful than beneficial is one that, on careful examination, I find difficult to sustain. While it is impossible to deny that computers carry genuine risks, the transformative benefits they have brought to virtually every domain of human activity are, in my view, far more substantial and wide-reaching.

On the positive side, computers have revolutionised medicine, education, communication, and economic productivity in ways that were simply unimaginable a generation ago. In the field of healthcare, computer-assisted diagnostics, robotic surgery, and AI-powered drug discovery have saved millions of lives and dramatically expanded the boundaries of what is medically possible. In education, the digital revolution has democratised access to knowledge on a global scale, enabling students in remote villages to access world-class lectures, libraries, and interactive learning tools. The economic gains attributable to computing technology — including e-commerce, remote work, and digital-financial services — have generated wealth and employment opportunities across entire economies.

Nevertheless, the risks associated with computers are real and must be acknowledged. Excessive screen time has been linked to sedentary lifestyles, attention deficits, and sleep disorders, particularly among children. The proliferation of misinformation, online harassment, and cybercrime represents a serious threat to social cohesion and individual security. Furthermore, the automation of routine jobs by advanced computing systems is reshaping labour markets in ways that carry significant costs for workers in traditional industries.

However, these harms are not inherent to computers themselves, but rather reflect the manner in which they are used. With thoughtful regulation, digital literacy education, and responsible design, the risks can be substantially mitigated while the benefits are preserved and extended.

In conclusion, computers are overwhelmingly more beneficial than harmful. The challenge lies in society’s capacity to govern and harness them wisely.

🛟 **3. BÀI MẪU B1 (~256 Từ)**

Nowadays, computers are used everywhere in our daily lives. Some people think that they cause more harm than good. However, I strongly disagree with this view. In my opinion, computers bring far more benefits than problems.

First of all, computers have greatly helped us in education and work. Students can access a lot of information and learning materials on the internet. Workers can communicate, create documents, and complete tasks much more quickly and efficiently using computers. Without computers, many jobs and activities would become much more difficult and time-consuming.

Second, computers have transformed healthcare. Doctors can use computer technology to diagnose diseases more accurately, perform advanced surgeries, and develop new medicines. These developments have saved countless lives and improved the quality of healthcare around the world.

Third, computers help us stay connected with people around the world. Through email, social media, and video calls, we can communicate with friends, family, and colleagues anywhere at any time. This has made the world a much more connected and accessible place.

However, I acknowledge that computers also have some negative effects. Spending too much time on computers can cause health problems like poor eyesight and lack of exercise. There is also the risk of cybercrime and internet addiction. But these problems can be managed with proper education and self-discipline.

In conclusion, I believe that computers are much more beneficial than harmful. As long as we use them wisely and in moderation, they will continue to improve our lives in many important ways.
