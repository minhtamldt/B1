Tổng hợp 20 Đề Ôn Tập

Bộ 20 đề luyện thi VSTEP Speaking Part 1

Chủ đề trọng tâm: Family, Childhood, Giving gifts, Hobbies (Collections, Reading newspapers/magazines)

Test 1

Let's talk about your family.

  • Do you live with your family?
  • How much time do you spend with them?
  • What activities do you usually do together?

Now, let's talk about your childhood.

  • Did you have a happy childhood?
  • What is your fondest childhood memory?
  • Who was your best friend when you were a child?

Test 2

Let's talk about giving gifts.

  • Do you often buy gifts for other people?
  • What kind of gifts do you usually buy?
  • On what occasions do people in your country give gifts?

Now, let's talk about hobbies and collections.

  • Have you ever collected anything as a hobby?
  • What are popular things to collect in your country?
  • Do you think collecting objects is a waste of money?

Test 3

Let's talk about your family.

  • Who are you closest to in your family?
  • Do you often share your problems with them?
  • How important is your family to you?

Now, let's talk about reading newspapers and magazines.

  • Do you often read newspapers or magazines?
  • Do you prefer reading news online or in print?
  • What section of the newspaper do you usually read?

Test 4

Let's talk about your childhood.

  • Where did you grow up?
  • What did you enjoy doing most in your childhood?
  • Do you miss your childhood days?

Now, let's talk about giving gifts.

  • Do you prefer giving or receiving gifts?
  • What was the best gift you have ever received?
  • Do you think the price of a gift is important?

Test 5

Let's talk about your family.

  • Do you have a large or small family?
  • Who does the housework in your family?
  • Would you like to live with your extended family in the future?

Now, let's talk about hobbies.

  • What do you usually do in your free time?
  • Why do you think people need hobbies?
  • Is it important to have hobbies that involve other people?

Test 6

Let's talk about your childhood.

  • What games did you usually play when you were a child?
  • Did you play outdoors a lot?
  • How have children's games changed since you were young?

Now, let's talk about giving gifts.

  • Is it difficult to choose the right gift for someone?
  • How do you choose gifts for your friends?
  • Have you ever given a handmade gift?

Test 7

Let's talk about your family.

  • Can you describe your family?
  • What is the best thing about your family?
  • How has your family influenced your personality?

Now, let's talk about your childhood.

  • Who was your childhood idol?
  • Did you prefer playing alone or with others when you were young?
  • What did you want to be when you were a child?

Test 8

Let's talk about giving gifts.

  • Do you ever give gifts to yourself?
  • What is the most expensive gift you have bought for someone?
  • Do you think gift-giving is important in relationships?

Now, let's talk about hobbies and collections.

  • Did you collect anything when you were a child?
  • What is the most interesting collection you have ever seen?
  • Why do people enjoy collecting expensive items?

Test 9

Let's talk about your family.

  • Do you prefer spending time with family or friends?
  • What do you usually talk about with your family members?
  • Do you and your family members often argue?

Now, let's talk about reading.

  • Do you enjoy reading books in your free time?
  • What kind of books or magazines do you like to read?
  • Do you think children today read enough?

Test 10

Let's talk about your childhood.

  • What was your favorite toy as a child?
  • Did you like going to school when you were young?
  • What is the most valuable lesson you learned in your childhood?

Now, let's talk about giving gifts.

  • What kind of gifts do children in your country usually receive?
  • Do you keep all the gifts you receive?
  • Have you ever received a gift that you didn't like?

Test 11

Let's talk about giving gifts.

  • Do people in your country prefer practical gifts or decorative ones?
  • How do you feel when you receive a surprise gift?
  • Have you ever forgotten to buy a gift for a special occasion?

Now, let's talk about your family.

  • Do you look more like your mother or your father?
  • Are family gatherings common in your culture?
  • How have your family's expectations affected your career choices?

Test 12

Let's talk about your childhood.

  • Did your parents spend a lot of time with you when you were young?
  • What was your favorite subject in primary school?
  • Do you still keep in touch with your childhood friends?

Now, let's talk about hobbies and collections.

  • Will you start a new collection in the future?
  • Is there anything you used to collect but no longer do?
  • How do collections help people learn about history or culture?

Test 13

Let's talk about your family.

  • How often do you have a family reunion?
  • Who is the most humorous person in your family?
  • What is the most memorable trip you have taken with your family?

Now, let's talk about reading newspapers and magazines.

  • What kind of news are you most interested in?
  • Do people in your country prefer reading news online or watching it on TV?
  • Can we always trust the information in newspapers?

Test 14

Let's talk about giving gifts.

  • Do you think it's better to give money or a physical gift?
  • Have you ever received a gift that was handmade?
  • Is it important to wrap a gift beautifully?

Now, let's talk about your childhood.

  • Did you have a pet when you were a child?
  • What foods did you dislike when you were young?
  • Were you a well-behaved child?

Test 15

Let's talk about your family.

  • Does your family have any special traditions?
  • What is the most important rule in your family?
  • Do you think parents should make decisions for their children?

Now, let's talk about hobbies.

  • Are there any hobbies you would like to try in the future?
  • Do you prefer relaxing hobbies or active ones?
  • How much money should people spend on their hobbies?

Test 16

Let's talk about giving gifts.

  • What kinds of gifts are suitable for a wedding?
  • Do you prefer buying gifts online or in a physical store?
  • Is gift-giving common in workplaces in your country?

Now, let's talk about reading newspapers and magazines.

  • Have you ever subscribed to a magazine?
  • Are fashion magazines popular among young people in your country?
  • Do you think printed newspapers will disappear in the future?

Test 17

Let's talk about your childhood.

  • What kind of stories did you like listening to when you were a child?
  • Did you often watch cartoons?
  • What is a childhood skill that you still use today?

Now, let's talk about hobbies and collections.

  • Would you like to collect art or antiques?
  • How do people organize their collections?
  • Can collecting things be a profitable hobby?

Test 18

Let's talk about your family.

  • Who makes the most important decisions in your family?
  • Do you think family structures have changed in recent years?
  • What is the best way to resolve family conflicts?

Now, let's talk about giving gifts.

  • Do you usually prepare gifts well in advance or at the last minute?
  • What gifts are considered inappropriate in your culture?
  • How do you react when you receive a duplicate gift?

Test 19

Let's talk about your childhood.

  • Where was your favorite place to play as a child?
  • Did you have a lot of free time when you were young?
  • Were your parents strict with you during your childhood?

Now, let's talk about reading newspapers and magazines.

  • Do you prefer sports news or entertainment news?
  • Are magazines usually more expensive than newspapers?
  • How do newspapers influence public opinion?

Test 20

Let's talk about giving gifts.

  • What is a popular gift to give to a teacher in your country?
  • Do you think giving gifts to colleagues is a good idea?
  • Should children be taught to make gifts themselves?

Now, let's talk about hobbies.

  • Is it easy for you to find time for your hobbies?
  • Do you share any hobbies with your family members?
  • Why do some people lose interest in their hobbies over time?
